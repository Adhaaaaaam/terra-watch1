Understanding the Connection

Vegetation Coverage

Vegetation is the area that is covered with plants like forests and jungles. Vegetation is so important for humans and the environment, as they decrease the temperature in hot summers, improve the air quality by absorbing toxic gases like carbon dioxide, and they are food and homes for wild animals. Without vegetation, the global problems like global warming and climate change would increase, which will harm the environment more and maybe make Earth an uninhabitable place.

Human Population

Human population has increased a lot in the last few decades. As a result, humans started building new cities and expanding their activities. You can see how human expansion on earth affects the vegetation and CO emissions in the maps above. We will explain the connection between them in the connection part below.

Carbon Monoxide

Carbon monoxide is caused by different human activities like riding vehicles and factory emissions. Carbon monoxide is harmful for human health because it binds to hemoglobin in the blood, reducing the ability of blood to carry oxygen. It also causes fatigue, headaches, confusion, and dizziness. It also affects the economy as people will not be able to work as hard as before.

The Critical Relationship

How They're Connected

The human population has increased rapidly over the last few decades. As a result, humans have started building new cities and expanding their activities. These two actions have caused two major problems:

1. Decreasing vegetation areas: As humans began building new cities, they cut down forests and destroyed lands that were suitable for farming so they could build on them.

2. Increase in carbon monoxide emissions: Carbon monoxide emissions have risen due to the expansion of human activities.

These two problems have combined to create an even greater one. When humans reduced vegetation areas, they destroyed the natural air filters that clean the air we breathe and make it suitable for life. It didn't stop there, as the continued expansion of human activities increased the concentration of carbon monoxide in the air. As a result, humans have unintentionally raised the percentage of carbon monoxide in the air we breathe, which is slowly harming them. If they don't take serious steps to solve this issue, their suffering will continue to grow day after day.
